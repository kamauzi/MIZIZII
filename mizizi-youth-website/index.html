About Mizizi

Rooted in community, built for youth well-being.

Founded in 2020 in Mombasa, Mizizi Youth Organization is a community-based organization created from grassroots conversations among young leaders who saw how stigma, limited support systems, and lack of accessible services were affecting the mental health and potential of young people.

Today, Mizizi works with youth, caregivers, families, women, men, and community partners to promote mental health awareness, safe support spaces, rehabilitation and referral pathways, social connection, skills development, and sustainable livelihoods.

Our Mission

To promote mental health, dignity, resilience, and opportunity among youth and communities through inclusive, practical, and community-led interventions.

Our Vision

A society where young people and communities can access support, speak openly about mental health, and thrive socially and economically.

Our Approach

We combine awareness, peer support, structured psychosocial interventions, referrals, SRHR education, talent development, and livelihood pathways.

Programs

What we do

Mizizi supports communities through practical, youth-friendly, and locally grounded programs.

Mental Health Literacy

Workshops and campaigns that increase awareness, reduce stigma, and provide basic coping tools for youth, caregivers, and community members.

Ustawi Minds Project

A mental health literacy initiative supporting youth, women, and elderly community members across wards in Changamwe Sub-County.

Problem Management Plus

Structured psychological support interventions that strengthen mental well-being and resilience among adults aged 18–64 years.

Ludo Mental Health Initiative

A play-based safe-space model using Ludo to support open conversations on mental health, masculinity, emotional expression, and peer support.

IPT-G Sessions

Interpersonal Psychotherapy Group sessions that help youth experiencing stress, anxiety, depression, and relationship challenges.

SRHR Program

Youth-friendly reproductive health information, digital referrals, menstrual hygiene support, HIV prevention, and GBV support linkages.

Youth Empowerment & Talent

Mentorship, skills development, career guidance, talent nurturing, entrepreneurship support, and livelihood linkages for young people.

YSLAs

Youth Savings and Loan Associations that link psychosocial recovery with financial literacy, savings, credit, and economic resilience.
